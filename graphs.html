School Shooting Analysis

Graphical Visualization

Description

This page allows you to view specific graphs highlighting trends on the number of school shootings in the data we have collected from the time period of 1990 to 2021. You will be able to choose from three graphs:


  • A timeline of the total shootings in the US
  • A bar chart of total shootings in the US grouped by area type
  • A bar chart listing the total incidents grouped by state

Total Shootings:

From the total shootings graph, we can see that there has been in incidents from 2005-2015, however it has dropped since then. However we cannot conclude too much from this data without looking at different factors. As such we looked at incidents at different area types and by state

Area Type Shootings:

In this data, we seperated each incidents in each year by an area type: Urban, Suburban and Rural. From this trend We can see that a high amount of incidents happen in urban areas, such as cities. We can also make the observation that rural areas are not as likely to have these kind of incidents We can show this further when we look at the visualization that groups these incidents by state

Total Shootings Per State:

As mentioned about the area type shootings, we concluded that most shootings occur in urban areas. We can further solidify this conclusion with state groupings. In the bar chart, We can see that California (despite its policies on gun control) have the highest shooting frequency, followed by Texas and Florida. These states tend to have areas of high population density such as San Francisco, Miami, Dallas and etc. If density or population has to do something with this trend, then factors that have a trend with population density (such as poverty rates, cost of living, etc.) have an effect on frequency

A Deeper Look:

While the analysis only analyzes area types, it opens avenues to look at more deeply. For example, we can ask more questions such as "does poverty have to do anything about it?" or "do policies have more an effect on the incidents". Future studies can also delve into why there was a spike in the periods of 1990-1995 and 2005-2015. We can ask ourselves what events may have caused these spikes, which can allow us to better put action into preventing school shootings.
